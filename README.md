# Metabolomics-of-JAL10-20-mutants-during-Ralstonia-infection-in-Arabidopsis.
Metabolomic profiling of JAL10 and JAL20 knockout and overexpression lines in Arabidopsis thaliana revealed infection-induced alterations in phenylpropanoid metabolism during Ralstonia solanacearum infection, supporting their role in defense-associated metabolic reprogramming.

**Heading**

Metabolomic analysis of functional mutants of  JAL10 and JAL20 to elucidate the response of Ralstonia solanacearum -associated metabolic alterations in Arabidopsis thaliana.


Arabidopsis thaliana, Ralstonia solanacearum, soil-born pathogen, , Root infection , Root Immunity

**Abstract**

To elucidate the functional role of endoplasmic reticulum–localized JALs, metabolomic profiling was performed using knockout and overexpression lines of JAL10 and JAL20 in Arabidopsis thaliana. Total metabolites were extracted from the roots of Col-0, jal10, jal20, 35S:JAL10#6, and 35S:JAL20#5 plants under both control and infected conditions at 48 h post-infection with Ralstonia solanacearum infection. Comparative analysis revealed significant alterations in the phenylpropanoid pathway in the knockout lines following infection. These observations were consistent with the glycoproteomic and phenotypic data, collectively supporting the involvement of JAL10 and JAL20 in modulating defense-associated metabolic reprogramming during infection.

**Method**

Arabidopsis thaliana seedlings Col-0, jal10, jal20, 35S:JAL10#6, and 35S:JAL20#5 were grown on Murashige and Skoog (MS) medium for 5 days. Seedlings were subsequently transferred to sucrose-free, reduced medium and inoculated with Ralstonia pseudosolanacearum, with mock-treated samples serving as controls. After 48 h of infection, root tips (~0.5 cm) were excised and immediately snap-frozen. Samples were then freeze-dried and homogenized in molecular-grade methanol containing 0.1% formic acid and 0.5 mM lidocaine as an internal standard prior to LC–MS analysis.

Chromatographic separation was carried out using an Agilent 1290 Infinity II LC System coupled with an Agilent 6545XT Q-TOF Mass Spectrometer (Agilent Technologies). Metabolites were separated on a C18 column maintained at 40 °C at a flow rate of 0.400 mL min⁻¹. The mobile phase consisted of water (solvent A) and acetonitrile (solvent C) with the following gradient program: 0–0.5 min, 2% C; 0.5–5.0 min, linear increase to 98% C; 5.0–6.0 min, maintained at 98% C; 6.0–6.1 min, returned to 2% C; and 6.1–8.0 min, equilibration at 2% C. A 5 μL sample volume was injected for each run, and the autosampler temperature was maintained at 4 °C.
Mass spectrometric detection was performed in both positive and negative ionization modes using a Dual Agilent Jet Stream electrospray ionization (ESI) source. The operating parameters included a capillary voltage of 3.5 kV, nozzle voltage of 1.0 kV, drying gas temperature of 300 °C at 8 L min⁻¹, sheath gas temperature of 350 °C at 11 L min⁻¹, and nebulizer pressure of 35 psig. The fragmentor voltage was maintained at 150 V. Data acquisition was conducted in Auto MS/MS mode across an m/z range of 95–1000, with the five most intense precursor ions selected during each cycle for fragmentation using fixed collision energies of 30 and 50 eV. Dynamic exclusion was applied for 0.30 min to reduce repeated fragmentation of highly abundant ions. Instrument operation and data acquisition were performed using Agilent MassHunter software.
Raw LC–MS datasets were processed in MS-DIAL for peak detection, alignment, and metabolite annotation. The processed data were further analyzed using MetaboAnalyst, where missing values were imputed followed by log transformation and data filtering to enhance data quality and achieve near-normal distribution. Multivariate analysis was performed using partial least squares discriminant analysis (PLS-DA) to examine variation patterns and sample clustering. Statistical validation was carried out through one-way ANOVA followed by Fisher’s post hoc test. To control for multiple testing, false discovery rate (FDR) correction was applied, and metabolites with an FDR ≤ 0.05 were considered significantly altered.
